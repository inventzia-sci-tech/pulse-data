# Commercial Licensing

pulse-data is available under a commercial license for organizations that
want to use the software without the obligations of the GNU AGPL v3.0.

This document is an **informational summary**, not the legal agreement. The
binding terms are in `LICENSE-COMMERCIAL.txt`.

## Why a commercial license?

The AGPL is a strong copyleft license. If you build a product or service that
incorporates pulse-data and is distributed or offered as a network service,
the AGPL requires you to release your derivative work under the AGPL — source
code included. The commercial license removes this obligation.

## What the commercial license includes

- The right to use, modify, and distribute pulse-data in proprietary or
  closed-source applications.
- Freedom from the AGPL's source-disclosure requirements (including the
  Section 13 network-use clause).
- Optional add-ons, subject to a separate agreement:
    - Email or Slack support, with named response targets.
    - Priority bug fixes.
    - Custom feature development.
    - Indemnification.

## How to obtain a commercial license

Email **operations@inventzia.com** with:

- Your organization name and country.
- A short description of how you intend to use pulse-data.
- Approximate deployment scale (number of users, instances, or production
